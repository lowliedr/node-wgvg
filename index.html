Fire Prevention Tips

Simple precautions can significantly reduce the risk of fire in your home and workplace.

Home Safety

  • Install smoke alarms on every level and test monthly
  • Keep flammable materials away from heat sources
  • Never leave cooking unattended, especially with oil
  • Maintain electrical systems and avoid overloading outlets
  • Create and practice a home escape plan with your family

Workplace Safety

  • Follow proper storage procedures for chemicals and flammables
  • Keep fire exits and extinguishers accessible at all times
  • Participate in fire safety training and drills
  • Report potential fire hazards immediately to management
  • Ensure proper maintenance of equipment and machinery

Outdoor Safety

  • Check local fire restrictions before outdoor burning
  • Keep campfires small and attended at all times
  • Extinguish fires completely with water, not just dirt
  • Dispose of cigarettes properly, never throw from vehicles
  • Maintain safe distance between structures and vegetation

Additional Safety Guidelines

Proper maintenance ensures your smoke alarms work when you need them most:

  • Test smoke alarms monthly by pressing the test button
  • Replace batteries at least once a year, or when the alarm chirps
  • Replace smoke alarms every 10 years, even if they seem to work
  • Keep alarms clean and free of dust and debris

A well-planned escape route can mean the difference between life and death:

  • Map all possible exits from each room in your home
  • Identify a safe meeting place outside your home
  • Practice your escape plan at least twice a year
  • Ensure windows aren't stuck and screens can be removed quickly
  • Teach children how to escape on their own in case you're not with them

Remember the PASS method when using a fire extinguisher:

  • Pull the pin to break the tamper seal
  • Aim low, pointing the extinguisher nozzle at the base of the fire
  • Squeeze the handle slowly and evenly
  • Sweep the nozzle from side to side at the base of the fire
  • Only attempt to extinguish small fires - if it grows, evacuate immediately

Emergency Response

Knowing how to respond quickly and correctly during a fire emergency can save lives.

1

React Immediately

  • Alert others in the building
  • Call emergency services (911)
  • Evacuate or prepare to evacuate
  • Only attempt to extinguish small fires
2

Evacuation Procedures

  • Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation
  • Cover mouth and nose with a damp cloth
  • Feel doors before opening - if hot, use alternate exit
  • Close doors behind you to slow fire spread
  • Proceed to designated meeting place
3

After Evacuation

  • Do not re-enter the building
  • Account for all family members/colleagues
  • Inform emergency responders of any missing persons
  • Stay at a safe distance from the building
  • Follow instructions from emergency personnel
